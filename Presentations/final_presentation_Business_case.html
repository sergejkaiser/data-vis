Summary Overall

  • Since the beginning of 2008 profit in the US declined, whereas orders increased year of year (YoY). In France both declined for 2008.
  • YoY difference in cumulative profits more than 1 mio. $. 2008 Profit growth slowed down since march.
  • Orders in 2008 Q2 high than in previous year, the revenue is nearly equal.
  • Profits are 21 % lower for bikes and 39 % lower for components.
  • From 2006 Q2 every quarter overall small loss in product categories Accessories and Clothing.
  • Orders per sales person of bikes and components in 2007 Q1-Q2 vs. 2008 Q1-Q2 show Linda Mitchell top seller of bikes. Increasing her strong performance.
  • Other high sellers as Michael Bythe and Jillian Carson show a strongly reduced number of orders in 2008 (rel. % reduction - 30-40%), other team members catch up.

Bikes Summary

  • Scatter plot shows one story why bike profits are reduced. Reduced orders of road bikes, which generated a lot of profit in 2006 Q3 - 2007 Q2.
  • Since 2007 Q3 profit and ordered quantities of road bikes decreased.
  • Introduction of touring bikes shifted market share away from road bikes.
  • Orders and profit from touring bikes not as high as road bikes.
  • Avg. unit cost increased in 2008 for touring, mountain and road bikes sold to the US and FR.
  • Avg. unit cost for sales to FR increased especially strong. Touring bikes and road bikes more than 50 % higher avg. unit cost.
  • Avg. unit cost of the five most sold bikes increased by 25 %.

Components- Avg. unit cost evolution

Components - Summary

  • Profit of components declined, whereas orders slightly increased YoY in 2008 Q1-Q2.
  • Detailed look at top 3 components versus the rest of the components, shows touring frames shifted market share away from road frames.
  • The largest share of profits is due to mountain frames.
  • YoY in 2008 Q1 road frames smaller share of profits. Road frames and touring frames together higher sales.
  • However in 2008 Q2 profits and sales of road & touring frames were smaller than previous year results.
  • Profit decrease in 2008 Q2 because of unit cost increase by 46% for Road & Mountain frames, 35 % for Touring frames
  • Other items avg. unit cost increased as well, but YoY 30 % decrease.

    Conclusion

    • YoY slowdown of profit growth since the third month of 2008. Both bikes and components, which are the main profit sources, show smaller profits compared to previous year.
    • The profits of bikes are reduced as avg. unit cost increased for both sales to the US and France.
    • Especially strong increase of avg. unit cost to France for mountain bikes and touring bikes.
    • Profits for sales to France in 2008 Q2 very low.
    • Components: Similar sales in 2008 as the previous year, revenue slightly lower.
    • Mountain frames main profit source for components.
    • 2008 Q1-Q2 strong increase of avg. unit cost for Mountain, Road and Touring frames, which are the three top ordered components.
    • Increase of avg. unit cost for components not different across countries.
